Example: Adding and Subtracting Fractions

Key skill video

\[ \dfrac{2}{7} + \dfrac{3}{5} = \dfrac{10}{35} + \dfrac{21}{35} = \dfrac{31}{35} \] \[ \dfrac{4}{9} - \dfrac{1}{4} = \dfrac{16}{36} - \dfrac{9}{36} = \dfrac{5}{36} \]

Method Comparison

To calculate \(\dfrac{1}{6} + \dfrac{3}{4}\), there are two methods

Multiply denominators \[ \dfrac{4}{24} + \dfrac{18}{24} = \dfrac{22}{24} = \dfrac{11}{12} \]

Lowest common multiple \[ \dfrac{2}{12} + \dfrac{9}{12} = \dfrac{11}{12} \]

Which method do you think is better?

Example: Adding Mixed Numbers

Key skill video

Calculate: \(1\dfrac{1}{4} + 3\dfrac{1}{12}\)

Improper fractions \[ 1\dfrac{1}{4} = \dfrac{5}{4} \] \[ 3\dfrac{1}{12} = \dfrac{37}{12} \] \[ \dfrac{5}{4} = \dfrac{5\times 3}{4\times 3} = \dfrac{15}{12} \] Add: \[ \dfrac{15}{12} + \dfrac{37}{12} = \dfrac{52}{12} = 4\dfrac{4}{12} = 4\dfrac{1}{3} \]

Partitioning

Integers: \[ 1 + 3 = 4 \] Fractions: \[ \dfrac{1}{4} + \dfrac{1}{12} = \dfrac{3}{12} + \dfrac{1}{12} = \dfrac{4}{12} = \dfrac{1}{3} \]

Combine: \[ 4 + \dfrac{1}{3} = 4\dfrac{1}{3} \]

Example: Subtracting Mixed Numbers

Key skill video

Calculate: \(7\dfrac{1}{12} - 5\dfrac{3}{4}\) Give your answer as a mixed number in its simplest form.

Improper Fractions

\[ 7\dfrac{1}{12} = \dfrac{7\times12 + 1}{12} = \dfrac{85}{12} \] \[ 5\dfrac{3}{4} = \dfrac{5\times4 + 3}{4} = \dfrac{23}{4} = \dfrac{69}{12} \]

Subtract: \[ \dfrac{85}{12} - \dfrac{69}{12} = \dfrac{16}{12} = 1\dfrac{4}{12} = 1\dfrac{1}{3} \]

Partitioning

Split into whole and fractional parts:

Integers: \[7 - 5 = 2\]

Fractions: \[ \dfrac{1}{12} - \dfrac{3}{4}= \dfrac{1}{12} - \dfrac{9}{12} = -\dfrac{8}{12} = -\dfrac{2}{3} \]

Combine: \[ 2 + \bigl(-\dfrac{2}{3}\bigr) = 1\dfrac{1}{3} \]

Example: Powers and Roots of Fractions

Squaring means multiplying by itself, so to square a fraction you simply square the numerator and square the denominator: \[\left(\dfrac{3}{7}\right)^2 = \dfrac{3}{7} \times \dfrac{3}{7} = \dfrac{3 \times 3}{7 \times 7} = \dfrac{9}{49}\]

The same goes for cubing: \[\left(\dfrac{2}{5}\right)^3 = \dfrac{2}{5} \times \dfrac{2}{5} \times \dfrac{2}{5} = \dfrac{8}{125}\]

To square root, you square root the numerator and square root the denominator: \[\sqrt{\dfrac{4}{25}} = \dfrac{\sqrt{4}}{\sqrt{25}} = \dfrac{2}{5}\]

Example: Dividing by a Unit Fraction

Consider a calculation like \(5 \div \dfrac{1}{3}\)

This means How many thirds go into five?

. . .

We know the answer is 15, but how can we explain the method?

. . .

We can explain this in terms of multiplying by the reciprocal: \[ 5 \div \dfrac{1}{3} = 5 \times \dfrac{3}{1} = 15 \]

Example: Dividing by Other Fractions

Key skill video

To divide by other fractions, we can use the same technique: multiplying by the reciprocal: \[ 4 \div \dfrac{2}{5} = 4 \times \dfrac{5}{2} = \dfrac{4}{1} \times \dfrac{5}{2} = \dfrac{20}{2} = 10 \] Donâ€™t forget to continue to cancel before multiplying if you can: \[ \dfrac{5}{12} \div \dfrac{3}{8} = \dfrac{5}{12} \times \dfrac{8}{3} = \dfrac{5}{\boxed{4} \times 3} \times \dfrac{\boxed{4} \times 2}{3} = \dfrac{10}{9} = 1\dfrac{1}{9} \]

Example: Dividing Mixed Numbers

Like with multiplying mixed numbers, the first step in dividing mixed numbers is to convert them to improper fractions: \[ 1\dfrac{3}{7} \div \dfrac{1}{5} = \dfrac{10}{7} \div \dfrac{1}{5} = \dfrac{10}{7} \times \dfrac{5}{1} = \dfrac{50}{7} = 7\dfrac{1}{7} \]

\[ 1\dfrac{5}{9} \div 2\dfrac{4}{5} = \dfrac{14}{9} \div \dfrac{14}{5} = \dfrac{14}{9} \times \dfrac{5}{14} = \dfrac{1}{9} \times \dfrac{5}{1} = \dfrac{5}{9} \]
