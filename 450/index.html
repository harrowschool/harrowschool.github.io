Archery

John Lyon directed in his will that Archery should be a compulsory part of the curriculum at the school. A competition for the Silver Arrow was held every year until 1771 when it was stopped because of crowd control issues, but the arrow still appears in Harrow School coat of arms. Since the mid-20th century, Archery has been taken up again as part of the sporting curriculum. Every year there is an annual match between Harrow School and The John Lyon School, in which we celebrate our association and membership of the wider Harrow family. To celebrate this unique sport during the Community Events, both schools are putting a historic display/competition where members of the public can enjoy seeing the boys in action shooting composite bows from 10 and 20 yards.
